B is the direct predecessor of C, the language of Jason's original code in the comic strip; the similarities are rather apparent. Where C is very flexible with its types, B (like its predecessor BCPL) is totally typeless; all variables hold machine words, which can be treated as integers, addresses, or collections of text characters. Nonetheless, it does have variable declarations, which specify the type of storage to use; extrn for symbols that come from outside libraries, static for globals, and auto for local symbols that go into the surrounding procedure's activation record on the stack.
