The language that taught an entire generation to program, BASIC began life as a compiled language on a timeshare system at Dartmouth. The use of line numbers for editing betrays its origins on a system with a teletypewriter interface; there was no screen to use for editing the source. The Microsoft founders turned it into an interpreter to fit it into the tiny memories of microcomputers, and for two decades almost every such computer shipped with BASIC built-in, often as the closest thing they had to an operating system shell.
